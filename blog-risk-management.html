Risk Management for New Investors

Sarah Jenkins
Senior Market Analyst
Oct 24, 2023
8 min read

Protecting your capital is just as important as growing it. This guide covers the fundamentals of risk management for new investors.

1. Position Sizing

Never put all your eggs in one basket. Determine the maximum percentage of your portfolio you are willing to allocate to a single investment. Proper position sizing ensures that a single bad decision won't devastate your entire portfolio.

2. Diversification

Spread your risk across different asset classes, sectors, and geographies. Diversification helps smooth out the volatility of your portfolio, as different assets will react differently to market events.

3. Have an Exit Strategy

Before you enter a trade, know when you will exit. Set a target price for taking profits and a stop-loss level for cutting losses. Having a predefined exit strategy removes emotion from the decision-making process when the market becomes volatile.
